# Chapter 2: Physics Simulation with Gazebo

This chapter explores how Gazebo serves as the primary physics simulation engine in robotics applications. We'll examine how Gazebo simulates fundamental physical phenomena like gravity, collisions, and dynamics, and how these simulations enable realistic robot testing environments.

## Learning Objectives

After completing this chapter, you should be able to:
- Explain how Gazebo simulates gravitational forces affecting robots
- Describe collision detection and response mechanisms in Gazebo
- Understand how dynamics simulation works in the context of robot motion
- Apply best practices for configuring physics parameters in Gazebo
- Design robot-environment interaction models for realistic simulation

## Prerequisites

Before starting this chapter, ensure you have:
- Completed Chapter 1: Digital Twins for Physical AI

## Introduction

Gazebo stands as the de facto standard for robotics simulation in the ROS ecosystem. Its sophisticated physics engine enables realistic simulation of robotic systems in complex environments, making it an indispensable tool for robotics research and development. This chapter will provide you with a comprehensive understanding of how Gazebo's physics engine works and how to leverage it effectively for humanoid robot simulation.
